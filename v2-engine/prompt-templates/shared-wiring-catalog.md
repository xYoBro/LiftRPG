# WIRE TYPES — MECHANICAL INTERACTIONS CATALOG

These are the types of connections between mechanical primitives. Select wires that serve your story&apos;s core tension. Each wire type describes HOW two mechanics relate — the specific mechanics involved come from your story analysis.

---

## THRESHOLD-GATE
**What:** When a tracker reaches a specific value, something new becomes available or changes state.
**Feel:** Discovery, revelation, escalation. The player earns access through accumulated action.
**Paper:** Printed as threshold annotation on the tracker + instruction text near the target.

**Pairs well with:**
- Fill clocks → map node unlocks (exploration)
- Heat tracks → archive section accessible (escalation reveals secrets)
- Progress tracks → new dice modifier becomes available (progression)
- Faction tracks → new encounter options open (choices mattering)

**Example printInstructions:**
- "When SIGNAL FIRE fills, cross off the seal on NODE 4. You may now route through it."
- "When SUSPICION reaches 5, read the next CLASSIFIED INTERCEPTS entry."
- "When RAPPORT fills, circle codeword TRUSTED."

---

## FEEDBACK-LOOP
**What:** Two mechanics that affect each other, creating oscillation or spiraling tension.
**Feel:** Difficult choices, risk management, push-your-luck. The player must balance competing pressures.
**Paper:** Paired instructions on both trackers, each referencing the other.

**Pairs well with:**
- Drain clock + heat track (spending safety raises danger)
- Resource + fill clock (earning resources advances a timer)
- Tug-of-war + faction track (shifting loyalty changes reputation)

**Example printInstructions:**
- "Each time you spend SUPPLIES, tick DESPERATION once."
- "When DESPERATION triggers, restore 2 SUPPLIES from the cache."
- "Each BREACH outcome ticks NOTORIETY. Each NOTORIETY threshold costs 1 GOODWILL."

---

## CONDITIONAL-ROUTING
**What:** Player state determines which narrative content they read — different tracker values or codewords lead to different story branches.
**Feel:** Agency, consequence, replayability. Your choices shaped a different story than someone else&apos;s.
**Paper:** Printed as conditional instructions in encounter text or on the HUD.

**Pairs well with:**
- Codewords → alternate REF branches (the designed-for pairing)
- Faction standings → different encounter narrative
- Heat thresholds → encounter text changes tone/stakes
- Tug-of-war position → different ally/enemy stance

**Example printInstructions:**
- "If codeword BETRAYAL is circled, read R32-X instead of rolling."
- "If FACTION standing is HOSTILE or worse, add suffix -H to your REF code this week."
- "If HEAT is 7+, the encounter text in brackets applies. Otherwise skip it."

---

## RESOURCE-CYCLE
**What:** Resources transform into each other or fuel mechanical advancement. Earning one thing costs another.
**Feel:** Economy, scarcity, meaningful trade-offs. Every gain has a price.
**Paper:** Dual-economy instruction on tracker sheet with explicit conversion rules.

**Pairs well with:**
- Dual resources (earn A, spend for B; earn B, spend for A)
- Resources + clocks (spending resource ticks a clock)
- Performance bonuses + mechanical costs (lifting well earns currency, currency spent on story advantage)

**Example printInstructions:**
- "Spend 1 INTEL to peek at any one sealed archive entry. Cross off the INTEL box."
- "When you earn FAVOR, you may convert 2 FAVOR into 1 INFLUENCE."
- "Each time you use ADRENALINE, step your RESERVES die down one size."

---

## ESCALATION
**What:** A mechanic permanently raises the stakes of another mechanic. One-directional pressure that only tightens.
**Feel:** Dread, inevitability, mounting tension. Things can only get worse in this dimension.
**Paper:** Threshold markers with permanent modification instructions.

**Pairs well with:**
- Heat tracks (they ONLY escalate — perfect source)
- Drain clocks (reduce starting value on reset)
- Encounter difficulty (later weeks feel harder because earlier mechanics compressed the space)

**Example printInstructions:**
- "Each HEAT threshold crossed: permanently cross off the lowest remaining SUPPLIES box."
- "When CORRUPTION triggers, reduce WILLPOWER clock size by 1 segment for the rest of the zine."
- "Each time ALERT rises, the die range for CONTAINMENT BREACH widens by 1."

---

## UNLOCK-CHAIN (Linked Clocks)
**What:** Completing one mechanic activates a new one. The game expands mid-play.
**Feel:** Progression, surprise, deepening complexity. The rules change because the story demands it.
**Paper:** "When X is complete, begin tracking Y" + new tracker space appears on a later page.

**Pairs well with:**
- Fill clock → new fill/drain clock (phase transitions)
- Progress track completion → tug-of-war activates (investigation becomes confrontation)
- Skill tree node → new modifier unlocked (character growth)

**Example printInstructions:**
- "When RECON is full, begin tracking ASSAULT on the Week 4 spread. RECON no longer advances."
- "When TRUST fills, the BETRAYAL drain clock activates. Shade its starting segments immediately."
- "Once all CIPHER nodes are checked, circle codeword DECODED. New routing rules apply."

---

## DESIGN GUIDANCE

**Start with 1-2 core wires that define the game&apos;s identity.** A feedback loop between the central tension creates the heartbeat. Then add supporting wires that branch from those core mechanics.

**Every wire should be narratively motivated.** Ask: "Why does this interaction exist in the fiction?" If the answer is "because it&apos;s a cool game mechanic," the wire is wrong. If the answer is "because the character&apos;s growing suspicion naturally makes the environment more dangerous," the wire is right.

**Avoid wiring everything to everything.** A web of connections is not better than a few strong ones. Two well-chosen wires that create genuine tension outperform six decorative ones.
